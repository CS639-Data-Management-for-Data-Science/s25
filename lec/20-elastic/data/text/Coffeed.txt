'''Coffeed''' was a "philanthropic artisanal coffee company" with its headquarters in [[Queens|Queens, New York City]], [[United States|USA]].<ref name="Crain's">{{cite news |last=Fung |first=Amanda |date=January 6, 2016 |url=http://www.crainsnewyork.com/article/20160106/NONPROFITS/160109960/a-local-coffee-company-grounds-itself-in-philanthropy |title=A Local Coffee Company Grounds Itself In Philanthropy |work=Crain's New York Business}}</ref> The company opened its flagship location in [[Long Island City]] in September 2012.<ref name="LIC Post">{{cite news |last=Murray |first=Christian |date=January 26, 2016 |url=http://licpost.com/2016/01/26/coffeed-to-open-new-lic-facility/ |title=Coffeed To Open New LIC Facility, As New York Expansion Continues |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160228230646/http://licpost.com/2016/01/26/coffeed-to-open-new-lic-facility/ |archive-date=2016-02-28 |work=LIC Post}}</ref>  As of June 2019, the flagship location on [[Northern Boulevard]] closed and the LIC Landing became the new flagship location.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.yelp.com/biz/coffeed-lic-flagship-long-island-city|title=COFFEED - LIC Flagship - CLOSED - Sunnyside - Long Island City, NY|website=Yelp|language=en|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coffeednyc.com/location/lic-landing-flagship/|title=LIC Landing – Flagship – COFFEED NYC|website=www.coffeednyc.com|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> By February 2016, Coffeed had a dozen locations in the [[New York City|New York]] area and one in [[Seoul|Seoul, South Korea]].<ref name="Crain's" />  Included among them are LIC Landing, an outdoor café in Hunter's Point South Park, and New Leaf, a restaurant in [[Manhattan|Manhattan's]] [[Fort Tryon Park]].<ref name="LIC Post" />  

Coffeed closed all locations and ceased operating in December 2019. 

== Business model, charitable partners ==

Each Coffeed location donated a portion of its revenue (usually 3-10%) to a charitable partner based in the same neighborhood as the store.<ref name="LIC Post" /> Former partners included [[New York Foundling|The New York Foundling]], [[New York Restoration Project]] (NYRP), City Growers, and Community Mainstreaming Associates.<ref name="LIC Post" /> As of 2016, Coffeed had donated nearly $250,000 to charity.<ref name="LIC Post" />
