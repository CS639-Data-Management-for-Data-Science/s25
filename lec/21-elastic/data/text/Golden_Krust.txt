'''Golden Krust Caribbean Bakery, Inc.''' is a [[Caribbean]] [[fast casual restaurant]] operator and manufacturer of [[Caribbean cuisine]] including [[Jamaican cuisine|Jamaican food]], [[Jamaican patty]], and other baked goods.

The parent company is owned by the Hawthorne family, and the stores are [[chain store|franchise]]d. There are over 100 Golden Krust restaurants operating in nine U.S. states: [[New York (state)|New York]], [[New Jersey]], [[Connecticut]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], [[Florida]], [[Maryland]], [[North Carolina]], [[Massachusetts]], and [[Texas]]. The majority of the restaurants are situated in New York.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goldenkrust.com/location |title=Store Search |author=  |publisher=Golden Krust Caribbean Restaurant |date= |accessdate=2016-07-10}}</ref> It is New York City's largest locally-owned restaurant chain.<ref name=NY/>

The company also distributes food products to retailers, schools and prisons, is considered the foremost Jamaican business in the U.S.<ref name="commend">{{cite news |date=August 30, 2006 |title=Shirley commends Golden Krust |url=http://www.jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20060830/news/news8.html |url-status=dead |work=Jamaica Gleaner |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071008202821/http://www.jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20060830/news/news8.html |archive-date=2007-10-08}}</ref> and was featured on the [[CBS]] reality television show ''[[Undercover Boss]]'' in 2016.

==History==
Golden Krust's origins trace back to a 50-year-old [[bakery]] owned by Ephraim Hawthorne in [[Saint Andrew, Jamaica]], that claims to serve family recipes.<ref>http://www.boj.com/articles/franchise/jamaican.htm {{Bare URL inline|date=August 2024}}</ref> Ephraim's son [[Lowell Hawthorne]], Golden Krust's former [[President (corporate title)|President]] and [[Chief executive officer|CEO]], opened the first U.S. restaurant in 1989 on [[Gun Hill Road (Bronx)|Gun Hill Road]] in the Bronx. To open its first restaurant, the Hawthorne family, according to ''Business Opportunities Journal'' pooled $107,000, "using the Jamaican concept of [[susu (savings)|susu]], whereby everyone pitched in $100 a week to raise start-up money after banks refused them a loan."<ref name="fast food">{{cite news |last=Aikman |first=Becky |title=Jamaican fast food: The next big thing? |url=http://www.boj.com/articles/franchise/jamaican.htm |url-status=dead |work=Business Opportunities Journal |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090602220458/http://www.boj.com/articles/franchise/jamaican.htm |archive-date=2009-06-02}}</ref>

[[Jamaica]]'s ambassador to the United States was present for the opening of the chain's fifth store in [[Atlanta, Georgia]] in 2009.<ref name="Jamaica">[http://www.jis.gov.jm/foreign_affairs/html/20090721T170000-0500_20487_JIS_GOLDEN_KRUST_OPENS_FIFTH_RESTAURANT_IN_ATLANTA.asp Golden Krust Opens Fifth Restaurant in Atlanta] Tuesday, July 21, 2009 Jamaica Ministry of Foreign Affairs & Foreign Trade</ref>

The parent company later became involved in the financial services industry{{how|date=March 2023}}.<ref>[http://www.jnbs.com/dynaweb.dti?dynasection=news&dynapage=news_media&id=188&PHPSESSID=fe21e59671d7fe5074ab92f27a2819e9 Jamaica National Overseas And Golden Krust (New York)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713111546/http://www.jnbs.com/dynaweb.dti?dynasection=news&dynapage=news_media&id=188&PHPSESSID=fe21e59671d7fe5074ab92f27a2819e9 |date=2011-07-13 }} February 18, 2005 Jamaica National Building Society</ref> Hawthorne came to New York in 1981 and graduated from [[Bronx Community College]] before working as an accountant with the [[New York Police Department]] for nine years. As of 2005, Nine brothers and sisters are involved in the family business;<ref name="N.Y.">{{cite news |last=Garcia |first=Michelle |date=February 14, 2005 |title=For N.Y. Caribbean Beef Patty Co., Business Is Cooking |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/politics/2005/02/14/for-ny-caribbean-beef-patty-co-business-is-cooking/1809c201-2549-42dd-95ab-9252b3697891/ |newspaper=The Washington Post |page=A03}}</ref> In 2016 there were a total 11 Hawthornes: Milton, Omar, Daren, Haywood, Lorraine, Lowell, Marcia, Herma, Velma, Lorna, and Monique.<ref name=NY>"Intelligencer: Tribes, Family Businesses," "[[New York (magazine)|New York]]," Jan. 25-Feb. 7, 2016, p. 15</ref>

Lowell Hawthorne was found dead of [[suicide]] on December 2, 2017, aged 57, inside a Golden Krust factory in the Bronx.<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Schrader|first1=Adam|last2=Parascandola|first2=Rocco|last3=Keith|first3=Ross|last4=Schapiro|first4=Rich|date=December 2, 2017|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/bronx/golden-krust-founder-commits-suicide-bronx-factory-article-1.3673215|title=Golden Krust founder commits suicide in Bronx factory|work=Daily News|location=New York|access-date=2017-12-03|language=en}}</ref> He was in debt, faced tax liens in excess of $150,000, and a class-action lawsuit.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lohud.com/story/news/local/2017/12/08/golden-krust-ceo-lowell-hawthorne-sued/934372001/|title=Golden Krust CEO Lowell Hawthorne sued by employees, less than one week after being found dead|work=Lohud|date=8 December 2017|last=Fitz-Gibbon|first=Jorge}}</ref> Still, his death was mourned by many including former employees, colleagues and Jamaican Prime Minister [[Andrew Holness]].<ref>{{cite tweet|number=937126930142261248|user=AndrewHolnessJM|title=My condolences to the friends, family and employees of Jamaica-born Lowell Hawthorne, CEO of Golden Krust Caribbean Bakery & Grill. ==Menu==

Their restaurants serve mildly spicy, and hot, [[Jamaican patty|Jamaican patties]], [[jerk (cooking)|jerk]] chicken and fish, [[dumpling]]s, steamed [[Yam (vegetable)|yam]]s, and curried meat dishes.<ref name="The Upper Krust">AJP page M3 May 8, 2009 India Abroad{{full citation needed|date=July 2018}}</ref> Items served include [[callaloo]] (Caribbean greens) and [[ackee]].<ref name="The Upper Krust" /><ref>{{cite web | last=Denitto | first=Emily | title=FOOD FINDS; The Revels You Know, The Revels You Don't | website=The New York Times | date=2005-05-01 | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/05/01/nyregion/food-finds-the-revels-you-know-the-revels-you-dont.html | access-date=2018-07-18}}</ref>

In 1998 the company produced 25 million flaky burnt-orange patties on assembly lines at its main facility.<ref name="Flaky">{{cite web | last=Waldman | first=Amy | title=NEW YORKERS & CO.; From a Flaky Foundation, a Food Empire | website=The New York Times | date=1998-04-26 | url=https://www.nytimes.com/1998/04/26/nyregion/new-yorkers-co-from-a-flaky-foundation-a-food-empire.html | access-date=2018-07-18}}</ref> The restaurants do a lot of take-out business, as the patties are portable, and also distributes to supermarkets in 30 states.<ref name="Flaky" /> Offerings include beef patty, vegetable patty, spicy beef and cheese patty, [[soy]] patty, [[oxtail]], [[curried goat]], [[brown stew chicken]], [[roti]] filled with curried meat or vegetables, and "coco" bread.<ref name="Flaky" />

==West Indian community==
The eateries are a draw to the [[West Indian]] community.<ref name="The Upper Krust" /> Many of its original franchises were opened by nurses, and many are opened near hospitals where many workers are West Indian with Caribbean heritage.<ref>{{cite web | last=Kramer | first=Louise | title=HOME FRONT; For Ex-Nurses, Real Money's in Takeout | website=The New York Times | date=2004-04-04 | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2004/04/04/jobs/home-front-for-ex-nurses-real-money-s-in-takeout.html | access-date=2018-07-18}}</ref> Core customers come from Jamaica, Guyana, Trinidad and Tobago, [[Barbados]], [[Haiti]], [[The Bahamas]], [[St. Lucia]], and [[Grenada]].<ref name="fast food" />

The chain started out serving Caribbean immigrants, but customers now includes Americans and immigrants of all national ancestries. The type of food served is "becoming more and more familiar to all Americans thanks to holidays taken in [[Aruba]], Jamaica, or [[Trinidad and Tobago]], or through meeting at work people from these islands and others."<ref name="The Upper Krust" /> By 2009, there were plans to open an additional 250 stores over the next five years in the U.S., [[Europe]], [[Canada]], and the Caribbean.<ref name="The Upper Krust" />

==Operations==
The restaurants do mostly take-out and have limited seating. The decor is decorated in bright "sunny" yellow and orange tiles.<ref name="fast food"/> Competing patty producers include Tower Isles Frozen Foods in [[Bedford-Stuyvesant]], which sells more than 100,000 patties a day to supermarkets, convenience stores, delis and pizza shops that bake them on site, and Caribbean Food Delights, which makes 250,000 a day at a factory in [[Tappan, New York|Tappan]], N.Y. "for sale at major supermarkets and warehouse chains, including Costco, Wal-Mart and Pathmark."<ref name="fast food"/> In August 2016, the company announced plans to move and consolidate all its operations to [[Orangeburg, New York]], near the Caribbean Food Delights location.<ref>{{cite web|last=Kramer|first=Peter D.|date=August 12, 2016|url=http://www.lohud.com/story/news/2016/08/12/golden-krust-plans-relocate-rockland/88580920/|title=Golden Krust plans to relocate to Rockland|work=lohud.com}}</ref>

The Hawthornes hope to add the Jamaican patty to the list of American ethnic fast foods alongside the [[Italian cuisine|Italian]] [[pizza]] slice, [[Jewish]] [[bagel]], and the [[Chinese noodle]].<ref name="N.Y." /> The company uses a rising sun [[logo]] and has a city contract to serve lunches to prison inmates and schoolchildren.<ref name="N.Y." />
